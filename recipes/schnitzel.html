Natasha's Pork Schnitzel Recipe

Schnitzel is a German delicacy that can be used with porkloin, chicken, or veal. If you have not tried it, they are super easy to make and of course delicous.

One of my favorite dishes to eat has to be schintzel. It's incredibly delicious and actually a ton of fun to make.

Below, I'll entertain you with not only the ingredients used to make this fabulous dish, but also the steps needed to prepare this delicacy.

Ingredients:

  • Two pounds of boneless pork chops
  • A third cup of all purpose flour
  • One tablespoon of garlic salt (can be made with equal parts salt and garlic powder
  • Half a teaspoon of parika
  • Half a teaspoon of black pepper, freshly ground is preferred
  • Three large eggs
  • Two cups of panko brea crumbs
  • Olive oil, canola oil, or any high heat oil to saute
  • Lemon wedges to serve with (provides a special dash of flavour)

Steps:

  1. Alright my friends, the first step is always to prep your workstation.
  2. Trim pork chops of fat and slice them into 1/2" thick cutlets
  3. Pound those bad boys (no not like that you sicko!) into 1/4" to 1/8" thick pieces
  4. Set up three bowls
  5. In the first bowl we are going to combine 1/3 cup of flour, 1 tablespoon of garlic salt, a half tespoon of paprika, and a half teaspoon of pepper
  6. In the second bowl, beat those eggs up!
  7. What goes in the third bowl Louis? Well, we've got those panko crumbs! Go ahead and pour 'em in there
  8. Now, we're going to take those meat cutlets and dip them in the flour
  9. Next dip the flour-coated cutlet into the beaten eggs, let the excess egg drip off back into the bowl
  10. Take that egg-flour meat contraption and dip into the panko crumbs to form a nice even crust
  11. Repeat this process to bread all of the cutlets
  12. Now add oil to a non stick pan, just enough to cover the whole bottom of the pan
  13. Fry those babies up! Should take about 3-4 minutes per side
  14. Wowza! you smell that! Bet you cannot wait to take a bite! I don't blame you but don't forget the lemons!
  15. Serve it up! Condiments can be mustard, ranch, ketchup, or pretty much anything!
